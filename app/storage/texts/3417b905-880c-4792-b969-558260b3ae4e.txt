Project Proposal: Project Claim-AI 
Subtitle: GenAI-Powered Catastrophe Claims & Insurance Accelerator 
Target Clients: Deloitte Financial Services Practice / Tier-1 Australian Banks (e.g., CBA, 
Westpac, Insurance Australia Group) 
 
1. Executive Summary 
In the wake of increasing natural disasters in Australia (floods, bushfires, cyclones), 
insurance providers are overwhelmed by "catastrophe peaks"—sudden surges of 
thousands of claims that break manual processing workflows. Project Claim-AI is a 
two-semester initiative to build a multimodal GenAI agent that automates the triage, 
verification, and drafting of insurance claims. By "seeing" damage in photos and 
"reading" complex policy PDS (Product Disclosure Statements), Claim-AI reduces initial 
claim assessment time from days to minutes. 
 
2. The Problem Statement 
Current insurance claiming is a high-friction process: 
• Operational Bottlenecks: Manual review of thousands of low-complexity 
claims during disasters leads to burnout and 4+ week delays. 
• Inconsistency: Different assessors may interpret "storm damage" vs. "flood 
damage" differently. 
• Customer Trauma: In disaster scenarios, customers need immediate certainty; 
waiting weeks for an initial assessment worsens the mental health toll. 
 
3. The Solution: Claim-AI 
A GenAI-driven platform that acts as a "Co-Pilot" for insurance assessors. It performs 
three core functions: 

1. Multimodal Ingestion: Extracts data from photos (e.g., a collapsed roof) and 
identifies the severity and type of damage. 
2. Policy Reasoning: Compares the visual evidence against the specific 
customer’s policy wording to determine coverage. 
3. Drafting & Explanation: Generates a "Decision Memo" for the human assessor 
and a plain-English explanation for the customer. 
 
4. Semester 1: Technical Architecture & Design 
Focus: Designing the "Brain" and the Guardrails. 
Key Deliverables: 
• Data Pipeline Design: A secure "Privacy-First" architecture that strips PII 
(Personally Identifiable Information) before sending data to an LLM. 
• LLM Selection & Benchmarking: Evaluating Azure OpenAI or AWS Bedrock 
(ensuring Australian data residency) for vision and reasoning capabilities. 
• RAG Strategy: Designing the Retrieval-Augmented Generation system to query 
thousands of pages of insurance policies. 
• Security Framework: Threat modeling for "Prompt Injection" (e.g., a customer 
trying to trick the AI into approving a fraudulent claim). 
 
5. Semester 2: Implementation & MVP 
Focus: Building the Agent and the User Interface. 
Key Deliverables: 
• The "Assessor Dashboard": A React/Next.js frontend where Deloitte Product 
Owners can see the AI's "Confidence Score" and reasoning. 
• Multimodal Agentic Workflow: Implementing a system where a Vision Model 
(like GPT-4o or Claude 3.5 Sonnet) analyzes images and passes findings to a 
text-based "Policy Agent." 
• Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) Integration: A system where the AI proposes a 
payout, but a human must verify and click "Approve." 
• Automated Audit Trail: A feature that logs exactly why the AI made a 
recommendation (essential for Australian APRA/ASIC compliance).
